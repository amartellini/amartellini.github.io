About

I study how emotion and cognition interact in the human brain. I'm currently a Master’s student in Cognitive Science at École Normale Supérieure (Paris), focusing on cognitive neuroscience and computational theory. I also work with Ev Fedorenko at MIT, leading an fMRI project on emotional prosody that spans experimental design, data collection, and analysis.

Previously, I spent two years in Mehrdad Jazayeri’s lab (McGovern Institute for Brain Research / HHMI), where I helped manage the lab's research operations and co-designed a research program investigating the neural and computational basis of embodied emotion. I graduated from Stanford University in 2023 with a B.S. in Symbolic Systems (Neurosciences) and a minor in Creative Writing (Poetry). I grew up in Biot, a medieval village in the South-East of France.

Through science and writing, I seek to describe the complexities of human behavior and make sense of my own experience of the world.
