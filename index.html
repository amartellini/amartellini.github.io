About

Hi! I study how language and emotion shape human experience. I’m currently a Master’s student in Cognitive Science at École Normale Supérieure, focusing on cognitive neuroscience and computational theory. I work part-time with Ev Fedorenko at MIT, leading an fMRI project on emotional prosody that spans experimental design, data collection, and analysis. Before this, I spent two years in Mehrdad Jazayeri’s lab (McGovern Institute for Brain Research / HHMI), where I helped manage the lab's research operations and co-designed a research program centered around brain–body interactions. I graduated from Stanford University in 2023 with a B.S. in Symbolic Systems (Neurosciences) and a minor in Creative Writing (Poetry). My long-term goal is to connect language, bodily signals, and appraisal to explain how emotions arise and influence cognition.

Research Interests

Language & affect (prosody, meaning, social context) • Brain–body interactions in emotion • Human neuroscience • Computational modeling • Robust, reproducible, open science.
