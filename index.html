About

Hi! I study how language and emotion shape human experience. I’m currently a Master’s student in Cognitive Science at École Normale Supérieure – Paris Sciences et Lettres, focusing on cognitive neuroscience and computational theory. I work part-time with Ev Fedorenko at MIT, leading an fMRI project on emotional prosody that spans experimental design, data collection, and analysis. Before this I spent two years in Mehrdad Jazayeri’s lab (MgGovern Institute for Brain Research / HHMI), where I helped manage the lab's research operations and co-designed a research program centered around brain–body interactions and emotion. I graduated from Stanford University in 2023 with a B.S. in Symbolic Systems (Neurosciences), with a minor in Creative Writing (Poetry)! My long-term goal is to connect language, bodily signals, and appraisal to explain how emotions arise and influence cognition.

Research Interests

Language & affect (prosody, meaning, social context) • Brain–body interactions in emotion • Human neuroimaging (fMRI) • Behavioral modeling • Robust, reproducible, open science.

Selected Projects

  • Emotional prosody in the human brain (fMRI + behavior). Designing tasks, running scans, and analyzing patterns that convey affect through prosody. (Fedorenko Lab, MIT.)
  • Embodied emotion & autonomic regulation. Helped shape a research program tying neural signals to physiological states; coordinated protocols, compliance, and multi-team operations. (Jazayeri Lab, MIT/HHMI.)
  • Bayesian modeling of risk behavior (BART). Built hierarchical models, ran MCMC on multi-cohort data (n>300), and evaluated 30+ metrics; presented at Stanford’s Symbolic Systems Poster Fair.
  • Precision oncology decision support. Prototyped generative and reinforcement-learning approaches for patient-specific treatment recommendations; shared results with cross-functional teams.
  • Origins of orphan globular clusters. Spectroscopic analysis and statistical classification; co-authored work presented at AAS 231.
